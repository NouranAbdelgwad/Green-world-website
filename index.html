Problem Summary

Egypt is facing major problems with water pollution and agriculture due to climate change, urbanization, and a growing population. The Nile River, which is the main source of water for the country, is in a dire state due to pollution and changing weather patterns. This pollution leads to the scarcity of clean water, causing illness and disease for us who use it for drinking and daily activities. Additionally, waste management issues would persist, leading to increased pollution and environmental degradation. Environmental pollution is causing grave and irreparable damage to the natural world and human society. A polluted environment is a polluted society, but we can reduce carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere and reduce waste on land and sea to create a better green world for all.

Our idea

Our innovative project is designed to help you lead an environmentally conscious lifestyle effortlessly. Our all-in-one solution is aimed at creating a clean and sustainable environment by focusing on water and agriculture. To get started, we are developing an AI-powered app that comes with a smart camera to monitor implant growth. This app rewards users with points for their efforts and educates them on recycling methods and organic farming tips. Additionally, in this app, we are creating a community-like social media platform to encourage users to educate and inspire each other on recycling and farming. We understand the importance of clean drinking water, and that's why we are also creating a smart product that helps users check the state of their implants. This product also tests drinking water for contaminants and alerts users if there are any issues. In the future, we plan to organize a real campaign where cars in every governorate will take non-biodegradable waste from users and exchange it for eco-friendly rewards such as implants, our smart device, or gift cards. With our project, we aim to make eco-friendliness accessible and rewarding for everyone.

Future work

Future work for the Project could include integrating with other eco-friendly products and services, expanding to other areas of sustainability, adding more gamification and social features, partnering with local environmental organizations, and including more educational content. A sustainable product marketplace could also be created, and green energy credits could be offered to incentivize the use of renewable energy sources. Additionally, educational resources for schools could be developed. It is suggested that each governorate could have a designated vehicle to collect non-biodegradable waste from residents, who would receive rewards for their participation.
